Charcoal-Grilled Ribeye Steak

Charcoal-grilled ribeye steak is a juicy, flavorful cut of beef with perfect marbling, seared over hot coals for a smoky aroma. This steak is simple yet indulgent, ideal for steak lovers and perfect for a weekend barbecue.

Ingredients

  • 2 ribeye steaks (1-1.5 inches thick)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Fresh rosemary or thyme sprigs (optional)
  • Butter (optional, for finishing)

Steps

  1. Take the ribeye steaks out of the refrigerator and let them rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat your charcoal grill until the coals are glowing hot.
  3. Brush both sides of the steaks with olive oil.
  4. Season generously with salt and black pepper. Add garlic and herbs if desired.
  5. Place the steaks on the hot grill over direct heat.
  6. Grill for 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare, or adjust time for your preferred doneness.
  7. If desired, place a small pat of butter on top of the steaks while finishing on the grill.
  8. Remove the steaks from the grill and let them rest for 5-10 minutes to retain juices.
  9. Slice against the grain and serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.
